I have been thinking about why most note-taking systems fail to compound. The usual explanation is that people do not review their notes often enough. But I think the real issue is structural — notes without typed relations are just files in folders, and folders do not create meaning.

The moment you name a connection between two ideas — this contradicts that, this supports that — you create something that a future version of yourself can actually reason about. An AI agent can traverse typed relations. It cannot traverse "I vaguely remember this was related to something."

Building this starter vault has made the difference visceral. The atomic note on compound knowledge systems and the literature note from Rivera's handbook genuinely disagree about whether compounding is passive or active. Declaring that contradiction explicitly means it will surface whenever either note is referenced. That is compounding in action.

The practical takeaway is simple: every time I capture a note, I need to ask not just "what is this about?" but "what does this relate to, and how?" The typed relation is the atomic unit of knowledge infrastructure.
