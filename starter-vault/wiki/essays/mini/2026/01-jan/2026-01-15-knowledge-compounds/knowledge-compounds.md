# Knowledge Compounds When You Name the Connections

Most note-taking apps promise you a "second brain." What they deliver is a graveyard of good intentions.

The problem is not capture. We are all excellent at saving things. The problem is that a note with no connections is just a file taking up space. It has zero compounding potential.

What changes everything is typed relations. Not just "this note links to that note" — but knowing that this note *contradicts* that one, or *supports* a specific goal, or was *derived from* a particular source. When every connection has a name, you can ask questions your future self will thank you for: "What contradicts my assumptions about X?" or "What evidence supports this claim?"

The hundredth note in a typed system is exponentially more valuable than the first. Not because the content is better, but because it has ninety-nine potential connection points, each with an explicit relationship type.

Stop capturing. Start connecting.
